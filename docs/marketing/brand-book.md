## 1. Brand Story

### 1.1 The locked one-liner (use verbatim)

> *Puente AI turns a trade document into compliance and payment routing in 15 seconds — for SMEs and customs brokers in the US–LATAM trade corridor.*

Locked in PRD §1 as of the 2026-04-29 v0.3 reconciliation. This is the company's load-bearing sentence. It appears verbatim on the website hero, in press releases, in deck title slides, and at the top of investor emails. Do not paraphrase in customer-facing or press-facing copy.

### 1.2 Tagline (locked)

> *Pay less. Move faster.*

From `BRAND.md` §9. Use as an accent line, image overlay, signature line, conference banner — never as a substitute for the one-liner.

### 1.3 100-word brand story

Puente AI is the trade intelligence layer for the US–LATAM corridor — both directions. Upload an invoice, a manifest, or a bill of lading, and in roughly 15 seconds the platform extracts the data, scores fraud risk, flags compliance gaps, and recommends the cheapest legal payment route. Two co-equal users on every shipment: Maria, the Miami-based bilingual SME owner, and Carlos, the licensed customs broker who clears it. We are broker-augmentation, not broker-replacement. V1 is a recommendations layer; V2 will execute settlement after the regulatory groundwork is filed and approved. Spanish is built in from day one.

### 1.4 Founder narrative (substrate, not copy)

The founder, Jay Alexander, was born in Amsterdam (EU citizen, Dominican heritage) and spent four years at goTRG selling US liquidation truckloads to Miami-based SMEs shipping into Bogotá and Santo Domingo. He watched immigrant business owners lose 3–7% per wire and a week per settlement, every single shipment. The MS in AI/ML at Northeastern Align is closing the technical gap. The Santo Domingo HQ vision is the strategic anchor — DR-CAFTA, EU-CARIFORUM, and Mercosur all touch the DR, and Puerto Caucedo plus Google's Digital Exchange Hub make it a legitimate Caribbean-basin AI processing hub. PRD §14 is the canonical version; quote from there for any About-page or press-bio work.

## 2. Voice & Tone

The tone anchor: **trustworthy like a bank, warm like a Miami neighbor.** That pair is the stress test for every sentence. If a line drifts to one side — too cold, too institutional, or too casual, too lifestyle — rewrite.

### 2.1 Lineage

Direct-response craft (Ogilvy / Bernbach / Schwartz / Halbert) for conversion copy. Brunswick / Sard Verbinnen / Joele Frank / Edelman Financial Communications discipline for any PR-adjacent or trust-building language. LATAM/US bilingual sensibility throughout — Spanish as a craft, never as a translation step.

### 2.2 Sentence-level rules

- **Lead with the strongest specific claim.** A number, a named scene, a verb. Never with "In today's fast-paced…" or any throat-clear.
- **Short and declarative.** Variable rhythm — a 5-word punch followed by a 20-word setup, then back to short. No 40-word sentences.
- **Verbs over adjectives.** *"Puente clears your shipment in 15 seconds"* beats *"Puente provides AI-powered shipment clearance services."*
- **Numbers must be specific.** "$1,200 less" beats "save money." "15 seconds" beats "fast." "20–50 active client files" beats "many clients." Round numbers that aren't actually round are a tell.
- **Headlines do 80% of the work.** Spend 60% of writing time on the headline / subject line / hook.
- **Plain language over jargon.** *"Fraud Score"* not *"Risk Assessment Matrix."*
- **Dollar figures over percentages, when possible.** *"You save $1,240"* beats *"You save 4.2%."*

### 2.3 Bilingual posture

- Spanish is written in Spanish, from a blank page. Do not translate English copy into Spanish at the end.
- Default register for SME audience: **`tú`** form, neutral LatAm.
- Default register for broker audience: **`usted`** form. Carlos is older, more formal, and that is the WhatsApp default in his segment.
- Preserve proper nouns and locations in their original language: *Tejidos Medellín*, *São Paulo*, *Doral*, *Santo Domingo*. Never anglicize.
- Supported languages per `BRAND.md` §9: EN / ES / PT.
- Spanish strings run roughly 25% longer than the English equivalent. Layout and email subject lines must accommodate.

### 2.4 Founder voice vs. brand voice

| | Founder voice (Jay) | Brand voice |
|---|---|---|
| **Person** | First-person, plainspoken | Third-person, claim-led |
| **Length** | Long-form OK on LinkedIn (300–800 words) | Tighter, evidence-anchored |
| **Anchors** | Miami / Doral / Santo Domingo / goTRG years; one observed scene | Locked one-liner does the heavy lifting |
| **Use for** | LinkedIn personal account, weekly investor update, About page founder section, podcast guest spots | Website hero, press releases, brand-account social, press kit |

### 2.5 Banned words (unless paired with a documented citation)

*revolutionary, disruptive, world-class, best-in-class, cutting-edge, game-changing, seamless, AI-powered* (overused — replace with what the AI actually does), *blockchain-powered, lightning-fast, supercharge, unlock, leverage*.

### 2.6 Banned product-context verbs (V1 honesty)

*send, settle, wire, transfer-on-behalf, move money, pay your supplier, settle the invoice, complete the transaction.* The product **recommends**; the customer **executes**. Acceptable verbs: *show, compare, flag, recommend, surface, score, route, verify, extract, explain, read, classify.*

### 2.7 Punctuation & format

- Em-dashes earn their keep for emphasis.
- No exclamation points.
- No ALL-CAPS except brand names: PUENTE AI, USDC, FinCEN, BCRD.
- Quotation marks for actual quotes only — do not "scare-quote" product features.
- Oxford commas on.
- Emojis sparing — one per post maximum, only if it does structural work. Acceptable: ✅ ❌ ⚠️ →. Banned: 🚀 💰 🔥 💪 🌟.

Two co-equal users on every transaction. The corridor is direction-agnostic. Every asset that names Maria should also acknowledge Carlos.

|---|---|

## 8. Image & Composition Style — "Gilded Meridian"

Visual philosophy from `puente-linkedin-philosophy.md` (referenced in `brand-profile-2026-04-29.md` §3): a meridian of trust rendered in gold and deep navy, with the diagonal as the sacred line of transit between origin and destination.

- **Editorial-clean, document-forward, scientific-visualization-adjacent.** Closer to *The Economist* and *FT Weekend* than to a SaaS landing page.
- **Document-as-hero.** An invoice corner, a customs stamp, a redacted line item, a packing-list grid, the texture of trade.
- **The diagonal is sacred** — the line of transit, the corridor angle.
- **Geometric elements** — circles, nodes, connecting lines, dot grids, thin luminous pathways — deployed with restraint.
- **One focal element per image.**

### 8.1 Photography

When used, photography lives in real working environments. Miami warehouses, Doral logistics offices, Port of Miami, Laredo border crossings, Santo Domingo back-offices. Diverse subjects reflecting actual SME owners and brokers — Latina women business owners, Latino brokers, mixed teams. Working hands, working desks, real documents.

Avoid: stock business-people, fake smiling, glossy lifestyle, conference-room cliché.

### 8.2 Illustration

Line-based, monoline, document-forward. *The Economist* / *FT Weekend* editorial illustration is the reference.

Avoid: 3D isometric scenes, bouncy 3D character mascots, corporate Memphis (squiggles, tossed shapes, primary-color confetti).

### 8.3 Data visualization

Clean and minimal. One comparison per chart. Routing-vs-baseline savings as a paired bar (wire vs. stablecoin) with the dollar savings called out in `text-emerald` and the baseline labeled. Always disclose the baseline (typically a 5% wire fee). Always show units. Tabular numerals.

### 8.4 Bilingual layout

When copy appears in an image, the design must accommodate Spanish strings ~25% longer than English. Do not crop the Spanish to fit an English-sized box. Either re-flow or trim the English so both languages breathe.

### 8.5 Explicitly off-brand

A third typeface beyond Funnel Display + DM Sans. Raw hex values used directly. Demoting the savings figure below 56px or out of emerald. Emerald as a background. Neon accents. 3D character mascots. Dollar-sign rain. Hand-drawn doodle aesthetic. Generic stock business-people. Conference-room handshakes. "Blockchain glow" imagery. Crypto-bro candle charts. AI-art uncanny-valley faces.

When in doubt: how would *The Economist* illustrate this if they covered LatAm trade fintech, with a navy-and-gold palette and a savings number in emerald? Default to that.

## 9. Copy Examples — Do / Don't

### 9.1 Hero

- **Do** — *Puente AI turns a trade document into compliance and payment routing in 15 seconds — for SMEs and customs brokers in the US–LATAM trade corridor.* (Locked one-liner, verbatim.)
- **Don't** — *Puente AI is the Stripe for cross-border trade.* (Investor shorthand only, never customer-facing.)

### 9.2 Value prop

- **Do** — *Save 4–5 days on your Miami → Bogotá settlement. We compare wire vs. stablecoin and show the dollar savings before you settle.*
- **Don't** — *Lightning-fast AI-powered seamless settlement.* (Three banned words, two violations of V1 honesty, one hallucination.)

### 9.3 Founder voice on LinkedIn

- **Do** — *My mom paid 7% on a $40K wire to her cousin in Santo Domingo last month. I built Puente AI so the next person doesn't have to.*
- **Don't** — *We're disrupting the $2.5T LATAM trade industry with revolutionary AI-powered blockchain settlement.* (Each adjective is a cliché. All four together are unforgivable.)

### 9.4 Press release lede

- **Do** — *Miami, FL — Puente AI today announced that its trade intelligence platform, in beta with [N] Miami-based SMEs and customs brokers across the US–LATAM corridor, has processed [N] invoices end-to-end in under 15 seconds each.*
- **Don't** — *Puente AI is excited to revolutionize the future of cross-border payments with the launch of its game-changing platform.* (Throat-clear, two banned words, no specific claim.)

### 9.5 CTA

- **Do** — *Analyze a document* / *Analizar un documento*. Verb-led, single action, names the actual product affordance.
- **Don't** — *Sign up today and save thousands on your wire fees!* (Promises money movement Puente doesn't execute, dollar figure without basis. Two compliance violations in one CTA.)

### 9.6 Customer reference (until KAN-22 yields permission-cleared quotes)

- **Do** — *A composite voice drawn from 30+ Miami trader conversations in Doral and Santo Domingo, 2026.* (Honest, attributable, low compliance surface.)
- **Don't** — *"Maria, a Miami importer, saved $1,200 on her last shipment using Puente."* (Composite-persona copy must be flagged as composite. Never imply a real customer testimonial that doesn't exist.)

### 9.7 V1 vs. V2 framing

- **Do** — *Recommends the cheapest legal payment route. Execution is your call. Direct settlement (V2) ships after we file FinCEN MSB and Florida MTL.*
- **Don't** — *Send money to your supplier in seconds.* (V1 honesty violation; we do not move money.)

### 9.8 Spanish — `tú` vs. `usted`

- **Do** (SME / Maria audience, `tú` form) — *Sube una factura. En 15 segundos te decimos qué falta y por dónde te conviene pagar.*
- **Do** (Broker / Carlos audience, `usted` form) — *Suba el manifiesto. En 15 segundos le indicamos las partidas, los faltantes y la ruta más conveniente para su cliente.*
- **Don't** — A direct translation of English copy. Spanish should sound like it was written in Spanish, not converted at the end.
